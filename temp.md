## The Multiplier

In real (inflation-adjusted) terms, the cost of replacing a headlight has gone up **roughly 10x to 100x**, depending on the vehicle. That's not entirely due to regulation or design complexity — modern LEDs genuinely last longer and illuminate far better than a sealed beam. But the *cost of a mistake* (a fender-bender that clips a headlight, for instance) has ballooned dramatically. A minor parking lot collision that would have cost $15 and ten minutes in 1982 can now generate a **$2,000+ insurance claim** — which is exactly the kind of repair-cost dynamic you were pointing to in your article.
